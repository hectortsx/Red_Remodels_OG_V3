#whatWeHandle

What's Included in a Basement Finish

🧱

Framing & Insulation

Stud wall framing, vapor barrier installation, and insulation to Denver's energy code — keeping your basement warm, dry, and comfortable year-round.

⚡

Electrical

Panel upgrades, circuit runs, recessed lighting, outlets, switches, and egress lighting — all permitted and inspected.

🚰

Plumbing

Rough-in for basement bathrooms, wet bars, and utility sinks. We coordinate with licensed plumbers for all below-grade and new fixture work.

🌡️

HVAC Extension

Ductwork extensions, egress registers, and supplemental heating and cooling to keep your basement as comfortable as the rest of your home.

🏠

Drywall & Ceilings

Drywall installation, finishing, and prime coat — plus drop ceilings or drywall ceilings depending on your height and access requirements.

🪵

Flooring

Luxury vinyl plank, engineered hardwood, carpet, or tile — all selected and installed for below-grade moisture conditions.

🚪

Egress Windows

Cut-in egress window wells and window installations required for legal bedrooms and fire safety in finished basements.

🎨

Paint & Trim

Interior paint, baseboard trim, door casing, and all finish details that make your basement feel polished and connected to the rest of your home.

Our Basement Finishing Process

1

Free Estimate

We walk your basement, assess ceiling height, moisture conditions, and existing mechanicals, then provide a detailed written estimate.

2

We pass the final building inspection and walk you through every detail. The certificate of occupancy is yours to keep.

#pricingGuide

Basement Finishing Cost in Denver

Costs vary based on square footage, finishes, and the complexity of the layout. Here's what Denver homeowners typically invest:

  • Basic finish (open layout, simple finishes)$30 – $50/sq ft
  • Mid-range (bedroom, bath, bar)$50 – $80/sq ft
  • High-end (home theater, custom bar, full bath)$80 – $130+/sq ft

A 1,000 sq ft basement typically runs $50,000–$100,000 depending on finishes and fixtures. Permits are required in Denver and are included in our estimates.

Ways to Save

  • Use an open floor plan — fewer walls means less framing and drywall
  • Choose LVP flooring over tile for a durable, cost-effective basement option
  • Skip the wet bar rough-in if you don't need it now — add it later
  • Use drop ceilings in utility areas to keep mechanical access without drywall
