Your bathroom is one of the most-used rooms in your home — and one of the best investments you can make. But before you start picking out tile, you need a realistic picture of what a bathroom remodel actually costs in Denver.

The short answer: **most Denver bathroom remodels run between $8,000 and $35,000**, depending on the size, scope, and finishes you choose. High-end gut-and-rebuild projects can push past $60,000. Here's exactly what drives those numbers — and how to stretch your budget without cutting corners.

## What Drives Bathroom Remodel Costs in Denver

### Size and Scope

The biggest cost factor isn't your tile choice — it's how much you're actually changing. There's a big difference between swapping fixtures and moving walls.

- **Cosmetic refresh** (new vanity, fixtures, paint, flooring): **$3,000–$8,000**
- **Mid-range remodel** (full tile, new tub/shower, updated plumbing): **$10,000–$25,000**
- **Full gut renovation** (layout changes, custom tile, high-end fixtures): **$30,000–$60,000+**

### Labor Costs on the Front Range

Denver's construction market is competitive. Skilled tradespeople — plumbers, tile setters, electricians — are in high demand across the metro area. Expect labor to account for **40–60% of your total project cost**.

Trying to lowball labor is the fastest way to end up with a botched tile job or a plumbing leak behind the wall. We'd rather give you an honest number upfront than clean up someone else's cheap work later.

### Plumbing and Electrical

If you're keeping plumbing in the same location, costs stay manageable. Move a drain or add a second vanity, and costs jump. Budget **$500–$2,000 for plumbing** on a standard remodel, and more if your existing pipes need updating — which is common in Denver's older neighborhoods.

Electrical costs depend on whether you're adding heated floors, a new exhaust fan, or GFCI outlets. Typical range: **$300–$1,500**.

## Where Your Money Goes: Material Choices That Move the Needle

### Tile

Tile is one of the most visible — and most variable — line items in any bathroom remodel. Budget tile runs **$2–$5 per square foot**. Designer tile can hit **$25–$50+ per sq ft**, not counting installation.

The good news: a skilled installer can make mid-range tile look incredible. The design and layout matter as much as the price tag.

### Vanity and Fixtures

A stock vanity from a home improvement store runs **$200–$800**. Semi-custom and custom options start around **$1,500** and go up fast. Fixtures (faucets, showerheads, towel bars) add another **$300–$1,500** depending on finish and brand.

Matte black and brushed nickel are popular in Denver right now — and they hold up well to Colorado's hard water.

### Shower vs. Tub

A tile walk-in shower typically costs **$3,000–$8,000** for a standard size. A freestanding soaking tub adds **$1,500–$6,000** to your budget before you factor in the plumbing rough-in.

Not sure if you should keep the tub? If you have kids or plan to sell within five years, most buyers in the Denver metro area still want at least one tub in the home.

## How to Get the Most Value From Your Bathroom Remodel

**Don't move plumbing if you don't have to.** Keeping drains and supply lines in place saves thousands.

**Invest in tile work, not just tile.** A creative layout — herringbone, large-format, contrasting grout — adds visual value without blowing the budget.

**Choose timeless over trendy.** Subway tile and clean lines age better than highly stylized designs. You'll thank yourself in ten years.

**Get at least three bids.** In a busy market like Denver, prices vary widely. The lowest bid isn't always the best deal — ask what's included and what's not.
